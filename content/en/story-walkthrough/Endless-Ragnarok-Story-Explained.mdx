## Unlocking the Epilogue and the Catalyst of Doom

To access the narrative content of the expansion, players must first complete the Proud Quest "The Tale of Bahamut's Rage" in the base game. Clearing this quest unlocks the Epilogue chapters, which serve as the entry point for the new story campaign. The narrative begins in Seedhollow, where strange dimensional tremors begin to disrupt the flow of ether. 

Lyria experiences severe headaches as her connection to the Astral Realm is violently tugged by an external force. Rolan’s residual ether signatures lead the crew to the Tredame Sanctum, where a massive gateway known as the Conflux has manifested. This gateway acts as a bridge between the Sky Realm and the deep boundary of the Astral Realm, leaking highly volatile mana that mutates local wildlife and threatens to collapse the surrounding islands.

As the crew investigates the Conflux, they realize that these rifts are not natural occurrences. They are deliberate tears in reality designed to draw the attention of the Heralds of Doom. The narrative progression is directly tied to completing high-difficulty Fatebreaker quests, which reward players with materials for [Weapon Transcendence](/en/weapon-transcendence/weapon-transcendence-guide/) and unlock further story cutscenes.

## The Heralds of Doom: Ragnalia and the Conflux

In **Endless Ragnarok Ragnalia explained**, we look at these entities as the central antagonistic force of the expansion's early chapters. The Ragnalia are not traditional Primal Beasts created by the Astrals to govern natural phenomena. Instead, they are autonomous defense systems native to the boundary layer between the Sky Realm and the Astral Realm. When the balance of ether between the two dimensions is disrupted, the Ragnalia activate to purge the "contaminants"—which, in this case, includes all life in the Sky Realm.

The Conflux serves as the staging ground for the Ragnalia. In the gameplay loop, the Conflux is a roguelite solo mode consisting of randomly generated rooms where players gather power-ups. Narratively, the Conflux is a shifting labyrinth of discarded memories and raw ether. As the crew descends deeper into its depths, they encounter the Heralds of Doom in various physical manifestations, culminating in boss fights that test the limits of the crew's combat capabilities.

Defeating the Ragnalia is crucial because their core crystals are being harvested by an external force to power a reality-warping engine. Players must learn to navigate their unique stagger-resistant shields and arena-wide ether storms. Utilizing high-tier characters like [Sandalphon](/en/characters/sandalphon-guide/) in Supreme Primarch Mode is highly recommended for these encounters due to his ability to bypass standard elemental resistances.

## The Chaos of the Astral Realm: Beelzebub and The World

The true mastermind behind the opening of the Conflux is Beelzebub, a fallen Astral whose ambition has long threatened the Sky Realm. In **Endless Ragnarok Beelzebub lore**, his objective is to use the chaos of the fractured boundary to usurp the power of the Creator. Beelzebub does not seek to destroy the Sky Realm out of malice; rather, he views both the Astrals and the Skyfarers as imperfect creations that must be swept away to make room for his own absolute rule.

To achieve this, Beelzebub manipulates "The World," a colossal entity associated with the Arcarum. The World represents the concept of completion and rebirth, but under Beelzebub's influence, its reality-warping powers are directed toward initiating a localized Ragnarok. By merging the core crystals of the Ragnalia with the power of The World, Beelzebub attempts to rewrite the physical laws of the Sky Realm, starting with the destruction of Seedhollow.

The climax of the story features a multi-stage boss fight against Beelzebub and The World. During these battles, the environment shifts dynamically, forcing players to adapt to gravity wells, temporal deceleration fields, and lethal light waves.

## The Role of the Six Dragons and New Allies

To combat a threat of cosmic proportions, the crew is joined by several powerful allies, including the Six Dragons and members of the Society. Fediel, representing the Six Dragons, plays a pivotal role in the narrative. As a manifestation of the element of Darkness, Fediel understands the decay of the boundary better than anyone. Her dialogue reveals that the Conflux is leaking a form of "void ether" that neutralizes the natural elements of the Sky Realm.

The addition of the 6 new DLC characters is integrated directly into the story quests:
* **Beatrix** and **Eustace** are dispatched by the Society to investigate the high concentration of Astral signatures in Seedhollow.
* **Fraux**, as a Pactbearer to The Devil, provides critical insight into the nature of The World, helping the crew understand how to disrupt its reality-warping fields.
* **Gallanza** and **Maglielle** act as frontline defenders, protecting the citizens of Seedhollow from the invading Ragnalia constructs while the main crew ventures into the Conflux.

These characters are not just optional roster additions; they participate in story cutscenes and offer unique tactical advantages in the new high-difficulty Chaos quests.

## Lore Connections: Granblue Fantasy Universe Integration

The **Endless Ragnarok story explained** in this article connects directly to the broader lore of the franchise, specifically the events of the mobile game's Arcarum: The Replicard Sandbox and the Astral Realm lore. The manifestation of The World is not an isolated event; it is the culmination of the Arcarum's attempt to replicate the physical world in a controlled environment. When Beelzebub breached the boundary, he hijacked this replication process, attempting to overlay the simulated reality of the Arcarum onto the actual Sky Realm.

Furthermore, the expansion introduces the Summon System, which allows Lyria to summon primal beasts in combat during Chaos-difficulty quests. This mechanic is lore-justified by the weakening of the boundary, which allows Lyria to draw upon the raw concepts of the Primals directly from the Astral Realm without needing their physical cores present. During Link Time, if all four party members execute their Skybound Arts, Lyria can trigger a Primal Burst, summoning controllable versions of Lucilius or Rolan to deal catastrophic damage to the enemy.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do I unlock the Endless Ragnarok story content?
To unlock the new story arc, you must complete the base game's main campaign and clear the Proud-difficulty quest titled "The Tale of Bahamut's Rage." Once this is completed, a cutscene will automatically trigger when you visit Seedhollow or the Tredame Sanctum, opening the gates to the Conflux.

### Who is the main boss of the Endless Ragnarok expansion?
The main antagonist of the expansion is Beelzebub, who manipulates the Arcarum construct known as The World to initiate a localized apocalypse. Players must fight both The World and Beelzebub in consecutive high-difficulty battles at the end of the expansion's campaign.

### Do the new DLC characters have their own Fate Episodes?
Yes, all 6 new DLC characters (Beatrix, Eustace, Fraux, Fediel, Gallanza, and Maglielle) come with fully voiced Fate Episodes that explain their individual motivations for joining the crew and their personal connections to the events of the Conflux.

### What is the canonical ending of the expansion?
After defeating Beelzebub at the Singularity, the crew manages to stabilize the boundary using Lyria's enhanced summoning powers. Beelzebub is sealed back into the dimensional rift, and the Conflux gateways close. However, the residual ether left behind permanently alters the weapons of the Skyfarers, unlocking the path to Weapon Transcendence and the Master Traits system.
