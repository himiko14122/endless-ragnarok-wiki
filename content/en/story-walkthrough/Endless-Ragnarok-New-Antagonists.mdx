## Ragnalia, the Herald of Doom

Ragnalia is the central figure among the Heralds of Doom, a faction of ancient entities seeking to collapse the boundary between the Sky Realm and the Crimson Horizon. In the lore of *Endless Ragnarok*, Ragnalia is depicted as a cosmic executioner sent to cleanse the skies of deviant primal energies. As the premier **Endless Ragnarok Herald of Doom**, Ragnalia commands immense light and dark elemental magic, shifting the battlefield into a void of pure chaos.

During the boss fight, Ragnalia utilizes a unique mechanic called the **Doom Gauge**. This gauge fills continuously throughout the encounter. If it reaches 100%, Ragnalia unleashes "Ragnarok Protocol," an unavoidable wipe mechanic that instantly defeats the party unless a Link Time is triggered or a Primal Burst is executed. In this **Endless Ragnarok Ragnalia explained** breakdown, we focus on the boss's unique phase-shifting loop and the specific counters required for each threshold.

### Phase 1: Herald's Call (100% - 70% HP)
Ragnalia begins the fight in their Light-aligned form. They hover above the arena, casting *Dawn's Edge*, which projects a grid of light beams across the floor. Players must stand in the safe zones (indicated by blue circles) while maintaining their DPS rotations. If hit, players receive the "Light Down" debuff, which increases damage taken from subsequent attacks by 50%.

### Phase 2: Abyssal Shift (69% - 40% HP)
Ragnalia drops into the center of the arena and channels *Abyssal Gate*, shifting their alignment to Dark. This creates three gravity wells that pull players toward the center. If a player is pulled into the center, they take massive dark elemental damage and are inflicted with "Slow" for 10 seconds. High-mobility characters or those with invincibility frames, such as [Sandalphon](/en/characters/sandalphon-guide/), can bypass these gravity wells entirely.

### Phase 3: Ragnarok Form (39% - 0% HP)
Ragnalia enters their true multi-elemental form. The Doom Gauge fills 50% faster in this phase. Ragnalia gains a shield that absorbs 5,000,000 damage. The party must deplete this shield to break the boss and stop the cast of *Ragnarok Protocol*.

## Beelzebub, the Chaos Bringer

Beelzebub, also known as the Avatar of Chaos, returns in *Endless Ragnarok* as one of the ultimate challenges in the Chaos-difficulty quest tier. His presence in the expansion is tied to his escape from the Pandemonium void, seeking to exploit the destabilization caused by the Heralds of Doom to usurp the Sky God's throne.

The fight against Beelzebub is a mechanical check of precision and timing. His signature ability, **Langallan Field**, creates a massive repelling barrier around him while sending out waves of black energy that inflict the "Chaos Rot" status effect. This debuff prevents healing and drains SBA gauges over time. To bypass the Langallan Field, players must either deal a specific threshold of stun damage or utilize ranged attackers like [Eustace](/en/characters/new-characters/) to pop the barrier's critical nodes from a safe distance.

At 50% HP, Beelzebub triggers *Anthem of Chaos*. This initiates a sequence where he teleports to the center of the arena, summons giant black wings, and begins charging an arena-wide blast. Players must execute a 4-chain Skybound Art (SBA) to trigger Link Time, which slows down the countdown and allows the team to break his guard. If Link Time is not achieved, the blast will deal 99,999 flat damage, instantly wiping anyone without a Guts or Auto-Revive buff.

## The World — The Arcarum Creation

The World is an entity born from the Arcarum, representing the culmination of the tarot-based arbiters. In the *Endless Ragnarok* campaign, The World acts as the gatekeeper to the deepest layers of the Conflux. To fully grasp **Endless Ragnarok The World lore**, one must look back at the original Arcarum project, where The World was designed to maintain absolute order through destruction. Unlike Ragnalia, who seeks to merge realms, The World wishes to reset the Sky Realm entirely to prevent its corruption.

The World's combat style relies on shifting between different tarot archetypes, each representing a card from the Major Arcana. Each archetype alters the battlefield's gravity, elemental rules, and player movement speeds.

### The Tower Archetype
During this phase, three massive stone pillars crash down onto the arena. These pillars continuously emit shockwaves that deal earth damage and stagger players. The party must split up to destroy these pillars. Destroying a pillar drops a blue ether orb that restores 25% SBA gauge to whoever picks it up.

### The Wheel of Fortune Archetype
A massive circular rune appears on the floor. It spins and stops on one of four elements: Fire (causes burn geysers), Water (spawns freezing glaciers), Wind (creates tornadoes), or Earth (causes localized earthquakes). Players must stand in the safe quadrants of the wheel while maintaining DPS.

### Judgement Archetype
The boss links the HP of all four party members. A green link line connects the characters. If one player takes damage, 30% of that damage is distributed to the other three players. However, the boss's defense is lowered by 30% during this phase, making it a high-risk, high-reward window for burst damage.

## Combat Strategies for Chaos Difficulty

Chaos difficulty in *Endless Ragnarok* represents the pinnacle of PvE challenge in *Granblue Fantasy: Relink*. Bosses in this tier possess massive HP pools and can easily wipe a party with a single unblocked hit. To succeed against Ragnalia, Beelzebub, and The World, players must optimize their builds and coordinate their party actions.

### Utilizing the Summon System
In Chaos-difficulty quests, the [Summon System](/en/summons/summon-system-guide/) becomes active. Lyria can summon primal beasts to aid the party in combat.
1. **Equip Summons**: Each party member can equip one summon passive, but only one player can field a controllable summon at a time.
2. **Lucilius and Rolan**: These are the two controllable summons available. Summoning Lucilius grants the party temporary invincibility and a massive attack buff, which is crucial during Beelzebub's *Anthem of Chaos*.
3. **Primal Burst**: Triggered when all four party members execute their Skybound Arts during Link Time. This inflicts massive elemental damage and extends the boss's break state, allowing for uninterrupted DPS.

### Sigil Optimization
Your sigil loadout must be tailored to survive one-hit mechanics while maximizing damage output.
- **Warpath+ (S-Tier)**: Enhances character-specific mechanics (e.g., Beatrix's Delta Clock acceleration or Sandalphon's Primarch mode duration).
- **War Elemental+ (S-Tier)**: Converts all damage to the enemy's elemental weakness, bypassing the need to constantly swap weapons for different bosses.
- **Alpha/Beta/Gamma (A-Tier)**: Essential for boosting SBA damage and cap limits, ensuring that your Primal Bursts deal maximum damage to skip difficult boss phases.

For more detailed build guides, refer to the [Best Sigil Setups for Chaos Difficulty](/en/sigils/best-sigils-guide/). For official patch notes and community discussions on boss strategies, visit the [Cygames Granblue Fantasy: Relink Official Site](https://relink.granbluefantasy.jp/en/).

## FAQ

### How do I unlock the fights against Ragnalia and Beelzebub?
You must first complete the Proud Quest "The Tale of Bahamut's Rage" in the base game. This unlocks the *Endless Ragnarok* epilogue campaign. Progressing through the story quests will lead you to "The Herald's Descent" (Ragnalia) and subsequently "Chaos Manifest" (Beelzebub) on Chaos difficulty.

### What is the Doom Gauge in the Ragnalia boss fight?
The Doom Gauge is a mechanic unique to the Ragnalia encounter. It fills over time during the fight. If it reaches 100%, Ragnalia will cast "Ragnarok Protocol," wiping the party. You must inflict stun damage, trigger Link Time, or use Skybound Arts to deplete the gauge and delay the wipe mechanic.

### Can I fight these bosses in multiplayer crossplay?
Yes. *Endless Ragnarok* supports full cross-platform play across PS5, PS4, Switch 2, and PC (Steam). You can form lobbies with players on any of these platforms to tackle Chaos-difficulty quests together.

### Which characters are best suited for fighting The World?
Characters with high mobility and strong area-of-effect capabilities are ideal for countering The World's tarot phases. [Sandalphon](/en/characters/sandalphon-guide/) is excellent for his aerial safety, while ranged characters like [Eustace](/en/characters/new-characters/) can easily target the monoliths during the Tower phase from safety.
