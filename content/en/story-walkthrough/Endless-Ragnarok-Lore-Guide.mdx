## The Astral War and the Cosmic Divide

The foundation of the Sky Realm's history is defined by the War of Separation, commonly known as the Astral War, which occurred approximately 500 years before the events of the game. The Astrals, a mysterious race possessing god-like powers and the ability to manipulate the essence of creation, invaded the Sky Realm from their home dimension. To secure their dominion, they constructed the Primal Beasts—colossal entities forged from the raw elements of the sky and programmed with specific administrative or martial directives.

Beelzebub, also known as the Chaos Bringer, stands as one of the primary antagonists of the *Endless Ragnarok* expansion. Unlike other Astrals who sought to maintain the status quo or govern the Skydwellers, Beelzebub sought to dismantle the boundary between the Sky Realm and the Crimson Horizon—a chaotic abyss beneath the clouds. His goal is to absorb the raw, unfiltered energy of the void to surpass both Astrals and the Creator. In the DLC, his presence is felt through the destabilization of the Zegagrande Skydom, prompting the crew to face him in high-difficulty encounters after completing the Proud Quest "The Tale of Bahamut's Rage."

The Astrals left behind structures and weapons that continue to dictate the balance of power. The six weapon types utilized by Skydwellers—Defender, Ascension, Executioner, Stinger, Stunner, and Terminus—are directly linked to these ancient designs. Unlocking Weapon Transcendence represents a player's effort to channel the residual Astral energy within these armaments, pushing them beyond their physical limits using materials harvested from Fatebreaker quests.

## Primal Beasts and the Summon System Mechanics

Primal Beasts are not merely monsters; they are living machines of divine engineering. Each Primal Beast was assigned a specific concept to govern, such as weather patterns, physical laws, or regional ecology. When the Astrals lost the war and retreated, many of these beasts fell into a dormant state known as crystallization, waiting for an Astral command or a significant surge of elemental energy to wake them.

In *Endless Ragnarok*, the Summon System allows Lyria to call upon these entities directly during combat in Chaos-difficulty quests and higher. This system represents a significant evolution in **Endless Ragnarok Primal Beasts lore**, demonstrating Lyria's growing capacity to act as a conduit for these massive entities. Players can equip up to four summons in their loadout, though only one can be active on the field at any given time.

```
[Lyria's Resonance] ---> [Equipped Summons (Max 4)] ---> [Fielded Summon (Max 1)]
                                                                |
                                                     (Trigger Link Time + SBA)
                                                                |
                                                     [Primal Burst Activated]
```

Among the most significant additions to this system are the controllable summons: Lucilius and Rolan. While standard summons provide passive stat boosts and a single, automated active ability, controllable summons allow the player to input specific commands, altering their attack patterns to suit the flow of battle. When all four party members execute their Skybound Arts (SBA) during Link Time, it triggers a Primal Burst, a devastating elemental chain reaction that infuses the active summon's element into a unified party attack.

For detailed strategies on optimizing your combat setup, refer to the [Weapon Transcendence Guide](/en/weapon-transcendence/weapon-transcendence-guide/).

## The Arcarum Connection and The World

The Arcarum: The Stars Shall Align is a major lore component of the Granblue Fantasy universe that takes center stage in the expansion's endgame. The Arcarum series of Primal Beasts represents the tarot cards, created by a rogue faction of Astrals who sought to destroy the existing Sky Realm and reconstruct it in a perfect, immutable image. These beasts are bound to human Pactbearers, individuals who share a symbiotic relationship with their designated Arcarum entity.

Fraux, one of the six new DLC characters introduced in the expansion, is the Pactbearer to The Devil. Her lore is deeply tied to the Crimson Horizon and the concept of burning away the impurities of the world. In combat, Fraux utilizes fast-paced melee attacks infused with fire magic, summoning the fiery fists of The Devil to execute heavy finishers. Her presence in the Zegagrande Skydom is linked to the appearance of "The World," a colossal boss that serves as the collective consciousness of the Arcarum system.

"The World" acts as a guardian of the natural order of destruction. In the *Endless Ragnarok* narrative, The World attempts to capitalize on the dimensional instability caused by Beelzebub to initiate a total reset of Zegagrande. Defeating The World requires players to understand its phase shifts, where it mimics the elemental properties of various tarot cards, demanding rapid adaptation and precise party coordination.

## The Conflux: Dimensional Gateways and Ragnalia

The Conflux is a core gameplay system introduced in the DLC that is deeply integrated into the **Endless Ragnarok Sky Realm lore**. Functioning as a roguelite solo mode, the Conflux is accessed via ancient gateways located in Seedhollow or the Tredame Sanctum. Lore-wise, these gateways are localized collapses of the boundary separating the Sky Realm from the Astral Realm, creating pocket dimensions where time and space are fractured.

Within the Conflux, players encounter Ragnalia, the Heralds of Doom. These entities are corrupted remnants of Astral defense systems, designed to purge any Skydwellers who attempt to cross the boundary. The randomly generated nature of the Conflux rooms reflects the chaotic, shifting memories of the Sky Realm stored within the boundary. As players descend deeper into the depths, the dimensional pressure increases, manifesting as harder combat scenarios but yielding rarer enhancement materials.

```
[Seedhollow / Tredame Gateways] ---> [The Conflux (Fractured Space)]
                                              |
                                     (Randomly Generated Rooms)
                                     - Combat
                                     - Treasure
                                     - Rare Monsters (Ragnalia)
                                              |
                                     [Deep Depths (High Rewards)]
```

Before entering a new area within the Conflux, players must select temporary power-ups. In the context of the **Endless Ragnarok world building**, these power-ups represent fragments of lost Astral knowledge that the player's character absorbs to temporarily alter their physical capabilities. These traits remain active until the player exits or fails the run, making strategic selection vital for surviving the deeper floors.

## Weapon Transcendence and the Fatebreaker Quests

Weapon Transcendence represents the pinnacle of gear progression in *Endless Ragnarok*, but it also carries significant lore implications. The process involves fusing fully enhanced weapons with raw cosmic energy harvested from Fatebreaker quests. These quests are high-difficulty trials where the crew faces simulated or resurrected historical threats, testing their strength against the memories of the sky.

Each of the six weapon categories has a unique lore origin that influences its Transcendence path:

1. **Defender Weapons**: Created by Skydweller kingdoms to protect their lands from Primal Beast incursions. Transcendence enhances their defensive wards, unlocking Slot II traits focused on damage reduction and health regeneration.
2. **Ascension Weapons**: Forged using materials blessed by benevolent Primal Beasts. Transcendence amplifies their elemental resonance, increasing the efficacy of active summons.
3. **Executioner Weapons**: Designed during the Astral War specifically to penetrate the armor of Astral commanders. Transcendence boosts critical damage and armor penetration.
4. **Stinger Weapons**: Precision instruments created by the Society to target the weak points of mechanical threats. Transcendence increases critical hit rate and exploit damage.
5. **Stunner Weapons**: Heavy armaments designed to disrupt the flow of mana within Primal Beasts, rendering them immobile. Transcendence increases stun power and link temp gain.
6. **Terminus Weapons**: Mysterious weapons of unknown origin that seem to share a connection with the Bahamut, the creator deity. Transcendence unlocks Slot IV traits, offering the highest damage output at the cost of specific status penalties.

```
[Fully Enhanced Weapon] + [Fatebreaker Materials] ---> [Weapon Transcendence]
                                                             |
                                                 - Unlocked Slot II/IV Traits
                                                 - Slot EX Rewards
                                                 - Aesthetic Effects
```

By completing Fatebreaker quests, players collect materials such as Astral Core Fragments and Transcendence Jewels. These materials are used to unlock swappable traits in Slot II (up to maximum level 25) and Slot IV (up to maximum level 10), allowing players to customize their weapons to match their specific playstyles.

For more information on the historical lore of these weapons, you can visit the official [Granblue Fantasy Portal](https://relink.granbluefantasy.jp/en/) for developer updates and official lore archives.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the connection between Beelzebub and the Arcarum in the DLC?
While Beelzebub and the Arcarum are distinct entities with different origins, their paths cross in the *Endless Ragnarok* expansion due to the destabilization of the Zegagrande Skydom. Beelzebub seeks to use the chaotic energy of the void to tear down the boundary of the Sky Realm. The World, acting as the governing consciousness of the Arcarum, views this dimensional instability as an opportunity to reset the world's cycle. This forces players to contend with both threats, as their conflicting goals both result in the destruction of the Sky Realm.

### How does the Summon System tie into the game's lore?
The Summon System represents Lyria's growing power and her unique connection to the Primal Beasts. Traditionally, Lyria could only manifest Primal Beasts she had personally bound. However, the dimensional anomalies in Zegagrande have thinned the barrier between reality and the memory of the sky, allowing her to tap into the essence of powerful beings like Lucilius and Rolan. This manifested energy allows players to control these summons directly in Chaos-difficulty quests, utilizing their abilities to turn the tide of battle.

### Why are the Conflux gateways appearing in Seedhollow and Tredame Sanctum?
The Conflux gateways are physical manifestations of the boundary collapse between the Sky Realm and the Astral Realm. Seedhollow and the Tredame Sanctum are locations of high magical and spiritual concentration, making them natural focal points for these spatial anomalies. The Heralds of Doom, known as Ragnalia, guard these gateways to prevent Skydwellers from accessing the raw Astral energy within, which players must harvest to progress their Weapon Transcendence.

### What are Fatebreaker quests, and why are they necessary for Weapon Transcendence?
Fatebreaker quests are high-level trials that simulate historical battles and cosmic threats. From a lore perspective, these quests allow the crew to test their limits against the memories of the sky, proving their worthiness to wield weapons infused with Astral energy. Mechanically, these quests are the primary source of materials like Astral Core Fragments and Transcendence Jewels, which are required to unlock the advanced Slot II and Slot IV traits on Transcended weapons.
