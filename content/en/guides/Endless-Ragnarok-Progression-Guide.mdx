The release of the major expansion DLC for Granblue Fantasy: Relink introduces a massive amount of end-game content, systems, and boss encounters. Navigating this new landscape requires a structured approach to maximize your efficiency. This **Endless Ragnarok progression guide** outlines the optimal path to unlock new mechanics, level up your roster, and prepare your team for the game's toughest challenges. Whether you are looking for an **Endless Ragnarok early game guide** to start your journey or trying to figure out **Endless Ragnarok what to do first** after clearing the base game, this roadmap will streamline your progression.

Before diving into the expansion content, you must complete the Proud-difficulty quest "The Tale of Bahamut's Rage" in the base game. Clearing this quest unlocks the Endless Ragnarok Epilogue, which serves as the gateway to all new mechanics, including The Conflux, Master Traits, and Weapon Transcendence. Once unlocked, you can access these systems via gateways in Seedhollow or the Tredame Sanctum. ## Unlocking the Expansion: What to Do First

Upon completing "The Tale of Bahamut's Rage," your immediate priority is to unlock the new progression systems and recruit the six DLC characters. You can acquire Crewmate Cards for Beatrix, Eustace, Fraux, Fediel, Gallanza, and Maglielle through specific side quests and trade-ins at Siero's Knickknack Shack. 

To systematically unlock the expansion, prioritize the following steps:

1. **Complete the Epilogue Quests**: Speak to Rolan in Seedhollow to begin the transition storyline. This unlocks the gateways to The Conflux and introduces the Summon System.
2. **Unlock the Conflux**: Enter the gateway in either Seedhollow or Tredame Sanctum. Clearing the first three depths of this roguelite solo mode is mandatory to unlock Weapon Transcendence.
3. **Recruit Core DLC Characters**: Use your saved Crewmate Cards to unlock characters like Beatrix (DPS) or Maglielle (Support) early, as they require significant leveling to match your main party.
4. **Unlock Fatebreaker Quests**: These high-difficulty quests are your primary source for Weapon Transcendence materials. They become available immediately after completing the Epilogue story.

| System / Character | Unlock Prerequisite | Key Feature / Reward |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **The Conflux (Solo Mode)** | Complete Epilogue Quest "The Whispering Gateway" | Roguelite runs, weapon upgrade materials, Sigils |
| **Weapon Transcendence** | Clear Depth 5 in The Conflux & Level 100 Weapon | Upgrade weapons beyond base limits, unlock Slot II/IV traits |
| **Summon System** | Complete "The World's Awakening" Quest | Field Lucilius or Rolan, trigger Primal Burst in Chaos difficulty |
| **Master Traits** | Reach Character Level 100 | Unlocks Insight, Essence, and Crux progression paths |
| **DLC Characters** | Trade Crewmate Cards at Siero's Knickknack Shack | Access to Beatrix, Eustace, Fraux, Fediel, Gallanza, Maglielle |

## Power Leveling to 100: How to Level Up Fast

Reaching Level 100 is no longer the end of character progression; it is the prerequisite for unlocking Master Traits. If you are wondering **Endless Ragnarok how to level up fast**, the answer lies in targeting specific high-yield quests and utilizing EXP-boosting Sigils. 

To optimize your leveling speed, equip your active party members with EXP Up V+ Sigils, which can be farmed from High-Level Griffins or traded at Siero's shop. The fastest leveling route involves a mix of the classic Slimepede quest and the new expansion-specific Fatebreaker preparation quests.

### The Slimepede Method
The "Slimepede" quest remains the gold standard for pure EXP and MSP (Mana Mastery Points). To maximize efficiency:
* Use ranged characters like Eustace or Rackam to quickly clear Slimes.
* Focus down Rainbow Slimes immediately when they spawn for massive EXP bonuses and Damascus Ingot drops.
* Run this quest in multiplayer to speed up clear times to under two minutes.

### The Fatebreaker Preparation Route
For players who also want to farm upgrade materials while leveling, the new "Heralds of Doom" preparation quests offer a balanced alternative. These quests pit you against Ragnalia's minions and reward substantial EXP alongside Weapon Transcendence materials.

## The Conflux and Weapon Transcendence Progression Loop

Once your characters reach Level 100, the core progression loop shifts to The Conflux and Weapon Transcendence. This loop is essential for preparing your team for the game's hardest bosses, including Beelzebub and The World.

The Conflux is a solo roguelite mode where you navigate randomly generated rooms containing combat challenges, treasure, and rare monsters. Before entering each area within a run, you select power-ups that remain active until you exit. Deeper runs yield better rewards but feature significantly harder enemies. Refer to our [The Conflux Solo Mode Guide](/en/conflux/the-conflux-complete-guide/) for room-by-room strategies.

Materials gathered from The Conflux and Fatebreaker quests are used for Weapon Transcendence. This system allows you to upgrade fully enhanced weapons (Defender, Ascension, Executioner, Stinger, Stunner, and Terminus) beyond their standard limits.

### Weapon Transcendence Priority
When upgrading your weapons, follow this priority list:
1. **Terminus Weapons**: Always prioritize these first. Transcendence unlocks Slot II (max level 25) and Slot IV (max level 10) swappable traits, vastly increasing your damage cap.
2. **Stinger Weapons**: Excellent secondary choice for critical hit builds, especially for DPS characters like Beatrix or Sandalphon.
3. **Ascension Weapons**: Upgrade these if you require additional HP and survivability to survive one-hit mechanics in Chaos difficulty.

For a complete breakdown of material costs, consult our [Weapon Transcendence Upgrade Guide](/en/weapon-transcendence/weapon-transcendence-guide/).

To upgrade these weapons, you will need to farm specific kits. Use this **Endless Ragnarok upgrade kit guide** table to target the correct materials:

## Master Traits and Summon System Integration

Unlocking Master Traits at Level 100 allows you to customize your characters across three distinct paths: **Insight**, **Essence**, and **Crux**. Each path features three unique Rank Perks and four Rank Style upgrades that alter how your character plays.

* **Insight Path**: Focuses on utility, cooldown reduction, and skill damage. Ideal for support-oriented characters like Maglielle or Cagliostro.
* **Essence Path**: Enhances basic attacks, combo finishers, and unique character mechanics (e.g., Beatrix's Delta Clock).
* **Crux Path**: Maximizes Skybound Art (SBA) damage, link time generation, and defensive capabilities.

At the same time, you must integrate the new Summon System into your loadout. In Chaos-difficulty quests, Lyria can summon primal beasts to assist in combat. Each player can equip up to four summons, though only one can be fielded at a time. Controllable summons like Lucilius and Rolan can deal massive damage and apply team-wide buffs. Furthermore, coordinating your party to use Skybound Arts during link time triggers a **Primal Burst**, dealing catastrophic damage to the boss.

| Master Trait Path | Primary Focus | Best For | Key Rank Perk |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Insight** | Skill Cooldown & Utility | Maglielle, Cagliostro, Io | *Quick Cut*: Reduces skill cooldowns on perfect dodge. |
| **Essence** | Combo Damage & Unique Mechanics | Beatrix, Sandalphon, Narmaya | *Flowing Blade*: Increases combo finisher damage by 15%. |
| **Crux** | SBA Charge & Link Time | Vane, Siegfried, Gallanza | *Link Dynamo*: Boosts link gauge generation rate by 20%. |

## End-Game Checklist: Transitioning to Chaos Difficulty

The final step in your progression is transitioning to Chaos-difficulty quests. In this tier, enemies like Ragnalia and Beelzebub can easily one-hit weaker characters, making proper preparation vital.

Use this checklist to ensure your party is ready:
* [ ] **Level 100**: All active party members must be max level with their Master Traits unlocked.
* [ ] **Transcendence Level 10+**: Your main DPS character's Terminus weapon should be upgraded to at least Stage 10.
* [ ] **S-Tier Sigils Equipped**: Ensure you have Warpath+, War Elemental+, and Character-Specific Sigils equipped on your primary characters. Learn more about these setups in our [Chaos Difficulty Boss Guide](/en/bosses/chaos-difficulty-boss-tips/).
* [ ] **Equipped Summons**: Equip at least two level-maxed summons per character for passive stat boosts.
* [ ] **Crossplay Coordination**: If playing multiplayer, utilize the full cross-platform play (PS5, PS4, Switch 2, PC) to team up with experienced players.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the fastest way to get Ragnarok Upgrade Kits?
Ragnarok Upgrade Kits are rare drops from the Fatebreaker: The World quest. You can also trade Gold Gold Dalia Badges at Siero's Knickknack Shack, though this is limited to three kits per week. Running The Conflux past Depth 25 also has a low chance of dropping them from gold chests.

### Can I use the Summon System in lower-difficulty quests?
No. The Summon System, including controllable summons like Lucilius and Rolan, is restricted to Chaos-difficulty quests and specific high-level Fatebreaker challenges. This restriction is due to the immense power of Primal Bursts, which would trivialize lower-tier content.

### Which DLC character should I unlock first for end-game progression?
For defensive utility and survivability in Chaos difficulty, Maglielle is highly recommended due to her flying weapon shields and healing capabilities. If you need pure DPS to clear damage checks against bosses like Beelzebub, Beatrix is the premier choice due to her high-risk, high-reward Delta Clock mechanic.
