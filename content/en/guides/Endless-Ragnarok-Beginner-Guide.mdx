## Unlocking the Expansion: Prerequisites and Requirements

To begin your journey in this expansion, you cannot simply jump in from a fresh save file. There is a specific progression path you must follow. First, you must complete the base game's main story campaign. After completing the campaign, you will unlock the Proud difficulty tier of quests at the Quest Counter in Seedhollow or Folca.

The absolute **Endless Ragnarok prerequisite quest** is the Proud Quest titled **"The Tale of Bahamut's Rage"**. This is a grueling encounter against Proto Bahamut that tests your team's DPS, sigil builds, and mechanical execution. Completing this quest unlocks the Epilogue of the base game, which directly bridges the story into the events of the expansion.

Once the Epilogue is complete, you will receive a notification that the gates to the new expansion content are open. If you are unsure about **Endless Ragnarok how to unlock** the new zones, head to either **Seedhollow** or the **Tredame Sanctum**. In these hub areas, you will find newly active gateways marked by swirling blue and gold energy. Interacting with these gateways allows you to enter **The Conflux**, the brand-new roguelite solo mode that serves as the core progression loop for the expansion.

Additionally, the expansion content scales heavily. While you can technically enter as soon as you finish the prerequisite quest, your characters should be at least Level 100 with fully upgraded weapons (level 150) to survive the initial stages.

| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 1 | Complete Base Campaign | Folca / Seedhollow | Access to Hard/Extreme Quests |
| 2 | Reach Proud Difficulty | Quest Counter | Access to Proud Quests |

| 4 | Complete Epilogue | Hub Areas | Access to expansion story |

## What to Do First: Your Initial Progression Checklist

Once you have gained access, you might feel overwhelmed by the sheer volume of new systems. If you want to know **Endless Ragnarok what to do first**, your primary goal should be unlocking the new character rosters and establishing a baseline farm. Knowing **Endless Ragnarok how to start** requires understanding the progression loop of the expansion.

First, claim your new DLC characters. The expansion adds 6 new playable characters, bringing the total roster to 28. These characters are **Beatrix**, **Eustace**, **Fraux**, **Fediel**, **Gallanza**, and **Maglielle**. You can recruit them using Crewmate Cards obtained through the new expansion quests or by purchasing them directly if you own the character pack. Each character brings unique mechanics: Beatrix utilizes the high-risk, high-reward Delta Clock mechanic to manipulate her attack speed and damage, while Maglielle offers outstanding support with flying weapons, shields, and healing.

Second, jump into **The Conflux**. This mode is a solo-only roguelite experience that takes about 20 minutes per run. It is the fastest source of enhancement materials in the game, including the materials needed for Weapon Transcendence. If you are following this **Endless Ragnarok beginner guide**, you should prioritize doing at least three Conflux runs early on to gather basic upgrade tokens.

Third, focus on upgrading your characters' **Master Traits**. Once a character reaches Level 100, you can unlock three distinct paths: **Insight**, **Essence**, and **Crux**. Each path features 3 unique Rank Perks and 4 Rank Style upgrades. Unlocking these requires Master Points (MSP) and specific materials dropped from Conflux runs.

## Mastering The Conflux: The Roguelite Solo Mode

For players looking at **Endless Ragnarok how to start** their endgame grind, **The Conflux** is where you will spend a significant portion of your time. This mode is accessed via the gateways in Seedhollow or Tredame Sanctum. 

In The Conflux, you enter alone with a single character. The mode is structured as a series of randomly generated rooms. Before entering each area, you are presented with three random power-ups (such as attack power increases, cooldown reduction, or life steal). You choose one, and it remains active until you exit or complete the run.

The rooms are categorized into Combat, Treasure, and Rare Monster rooms. Combat rooms require you to defeat waves of enemies within a time limit. Treasure rooms contain chests with valuable Sigils and upgrade materials. Rare Monster rooms pit you against elusive foes like the Golden Slime, which drop massive amounts of Master Points and rare upgrade items.

As you descend deeper into the depths, the difficulty scales exponentially. However, the quality of the rewards scales alongside it. A successful run takes approximately 20 minutes and rewards you with Weapon Transcendence materials, Master Trait tokens, and S-tier Sigils.

If you struggle to clear the deeper depths, consider using high-sustain characters. **Siegfried** and **Vane** are excellent choices due to their defensive buffs and life-steal capabilities. Alternatively, the SS-tier character **Sandalphon** can easily clear rooms using his Supreme Primarch Mode, which unleashes devastating lightstep sword waves.

| Room Type | Objective | Risk Level | Primary Rewards |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Combat Room | Defeat all enemies within the time limit | Medium | Weapon Materials, EXP |
| Treasure Room | Open chests and avoid environmental hazards | Low | Gold, Sigil Upgrade Materials |
| Rare Monster | Defeat rare spawns (e.g., Rainbow Slimes) | High | Master Points (MSP), Conflux Tokens |
| Gateway Boss | Defeat a major boss (e.g., Ragnalia) | Very High | Transcendence Materials, S-tier Sigils |

## Weapon Transcendence and the Summon System

Once you have established a steady flow of materials from The Conflux and the new **Fatebreaker quests**, you can begin upgrading your weapons via **Weapon Transcendence**. This system allows you to push your fully enhanced weapons (Level 150) beyond their standard limits. When considering **Endless Ragnarok what to do first** with your Transcendence materials, prioritize your primary DPS character's Terminus or Stinger weapon.

There are 6 weapon types eligible for Transcendence: Defender, Ascension, Executioner, Stinger, Stunner, and Terminus. Transcending a weapon unlocks swappable traits. Slot II can be upgraded up to a maximum of Level 25, while Slot IV can reach up to Level 10. Additionally, achieving Weapon Transcendence unlocks exclusive Slot EX rewards and unique aesthetic weapon glows.

Another major mechanic introduced in the expansion is the **Summon System**. In Chaos-difficulty quests and higher, Lyria can summon powerful primal beasts to aid you in combat. Each player can bring up to 4 summons in their loadout, but only 1 can be active on the field at a time.

Two summons are fully controllable: **Lucilius** and **Rolan**. When summoned, they replace your active character temporarily, allowing you to execute devastating unique attacks. Furthermore, if all 4 party members activate their Skybound Arts (SBA) during Link Time while a summon is active, it triggers a **Primal Burst**, dealing millions of points of damage and applying a prolonged debuff to the boss.

Even when not actively summoned, equipped primal beasts provide passive stat bonuses to your characters, such as increased critical damage or auto-revive.

## Surviving Chaos Difficulty and Optimizing Sigils

The final step in your **Endless Ragnarok beginner guide** journey is preparing for **Chaos difficulty**. This is the highest difficulty tier in the game, where bosses like **Beelzebub**, **The World**, and **Ragnalia** can easily one-hit KO characters who lack proper defensive setups. While this guide explains **Endless Ragnarok how to unlock** the core features, surviving them requires a complete overhaul of your loadout.

To survive, you must optimize your Sigil builds. The expansion introduces new S-tier and A-tier Sigils that are essential for tackling Chaos difficulty quests.

*   **S-Tier Sigils**: Look for **Warpath+** and **War Elemental+**. Warpath+ Sigils are character-specific and provide massive boosts to their unique mechanics (for example, Sandalphon's Warpath+ increases the duration of his Supreme Primarch Mode). War Elemental+ ensures all your attacks deal elemental weakness damage, regardless of the enemy's element, while providing secondary defensive stats.
*   **A-Tier Sigils**: Focus on **Critical Hit Rate V+** and the **Alpha/Beta/Gamma** Sigils. These Sigils allow you to reach the damage cap while boosting your Skybound Art damage and link level generation.

When configuring your build, do not neglect defensive options. Running at least one level of **Autorevive** or **Guts** is mandatory in Chaos difficulty to prevent instant failure from boss mechanics.

| War Elemental+ | S | Deals weakness damage + Random defensive stat | The Conflux (Deep Depths) |
| Alpha V+ | A | SBA Damage Cap Up + SBA Gauge Gain | Chaos Difficulty Bosses |
| Gamma V+ | A | Shield on SBA activation + Attack Cap Up | Beelzebub Raid |

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### What is the fastest way to unlock the Endless Ragnarok expansion?
To access the expansion, you must complete the main story of *Granblue Fantasy: Relink*, unlock Proud difficulty at the Quest Counter, and clear the quest "The Tale of Bahamut's Rage". This serves as the core **Endless Ragnarok prerequisite quest** chain that unlocks the Epilogue.

### Can I play the new expansion content in co-op mode?
Yes. The expansion features full cross-platform play (crossplay) across PS5, PS4, Switch 2, and PC (Steam). You can team up with other players for all Chaos-difficulty quests, Fatebreaker quests, and boss raids. However, **The Conflux** is strictly a solo-only roguelite mode.

### Which of the new DLC characters is the easiest for beginners?
Maglielle is highly recommended for beginners. Her floating weapons allow her to deal consistent damage from a safe distance, and her ability to generate shields and heals provides excellent survivability for solo content like The Conflux.

### How do I get materials for Weapon Transcendence?
Weapon Transcendence materials are primarily sourced from completing **Fatebreaker quests** and clearing deep floors of **The Conflux**. You will need to defeat high-level bosses like Ragnalia and Beelzebub to get the rarest drops.
