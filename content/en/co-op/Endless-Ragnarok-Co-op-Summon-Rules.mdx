## The Core Mechanics of Multiplayer Summoning

In Chaos-difficulty quests and Fatebreaker quests, each of the four players can equip up to 4 passive summons in their loadout. However, only one player can actively field a summon at any given moment. This restriction is known as the **Endless Ragnarok one summon at a time co op** rule. If Player A summons Bahamut, Player B's summon prompt will be greyed out until Bahamut's active phase ends or is prematurely dismissed. 

Furthermore, the **Endless Ragnarok summon per player co op** limit restricts each individual to a specific Summon Action Point (SAP) pool. You cannot summon back-to-back without regenerating your summon gauge via dealing damage, executing Link Attacks, or triggering SBA (Skybound Arts). 

To initiate a summon, a player must fill their individual Summon Gauge to 100%. Once filled, pressing the designated summon shortcut (L3 + R3 by default on PS5 and Switch 2) triggers Lyria's summoning animation. During this brief 1.5-second animation, the summoning player is invulnerable, but they cannot move or attack.

| **Rolan** (Controllable) | 150 | 20 | 240 | Player controls Rolan; provides party-wide barrier and attack buffs |

Managing these cooldowns requires open communication. If your team is running a high-difficulty Fatebreaker quest, designating a "Summon Shotcaller" ensures that utility summons like Colossus Omega are not wasted during transition phases where the boss is invulnerable.

## The Subsequent Summon Discount Mechanic

While the restriction of having only one active summon at a time might seem limiting, Cygames designed a mechanic to encourage rapid, sequential summoning. Under the rule that **Endless Ragnarok subsequent summons cost less**, triggering a summon immediately after another player's summon finishes grants a significant SAP discount to the next player in the queue.

This discount window is brief—lasting exactly 5 seconds after the previous summon exits the field. If a teammate initiates their summon within this 5-second "Resonance Window," their SAP cost is reduced. If a third and fourth player follow up in rapid succession, the discount scales even higher, allowing for devastating summon chains.

| Chain Tier | Resonance Window (Seconds) | SAP Cost Reduction (%) | Bonus Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Chain 1 (Initiator)** | N/A (Base Summon) | 0% | None |
| **Chain 2 (Follow-up)** | 5.0 | 20% | Extends active summon duration by 2 seconds |
| **Chain 3 (Follow-up)** | 5.0 | 35% | Boosts summon skill damage by 15% |
| **Chain 4 (Cap Stone)** | 5.0 | 50% | Instantly recovers 10% SBA gauge for the entire party |

To execute this strategy successfully in co-op, players must watch the active summon's duration bar located just below the boss's health bar. As the bar approaches zero, the next player in the rotation should prepare to input their summon command. This is particularly useful when attempting to break the shield of bosses like Ragnalia, where sustained elemental pressure is required.

## Controllable Summons: Playing as Lucilius and Rolan

Unlike standard passive summons that perform a single strike and depart, Lucilius and Rolan are fully controllable units. When a player summons either of these legendary figures, their character is temporarily replaced on the field. The summoner gains direct control of the primal beast, utilizing a unique set of high-damage skills and defensive maneuvers.

During this time, the other three players must protect the summoner's physical body, which remains vulnerable in a meditative state unless protected by support characters like Maglielle (who can deploy shields and flying weapons) or Vane (with his Rampart shield).

```
[Player Summons Lucilius/Rolan] 
       │
       ├─► Summoner's Body: Vulnerable (Requires protection from teammates)
       │
       └─► Summon Control: Active (20-second duration)
             ├─► Basic Attacks: Projectile waves / Sweep attacks
             ├─► Skill 1: High-damage AoE (Cooldown-based)
```

Controllable summons alter the flow of combat significantly. While controlling Lucilius, the player can spam lightstep sword waves similar to [Sandalphon](/en/characters/sandalphon-guide/) in Supreme Primarch Mode. This makes Lucilius an SS-tier summon choice for pure DPS phases. Rolan, on the other hand, is best deployed during intense boss overdrive phases, as his defensive barriers can absorb one-hit kill mechanics from bosses like Beelzebub.

## Link Time Coordination and Primal Burst Execution

Maximizing your damage output in Chaos-difficulty quests requires aligning your summon windows with Link Time. The **Endless Ragnarok co op link attack timing** is critical here. When the Link Gauge reaches 100% and all players perform a coordinated Link Attack, the party enters Link Time. During Link Time, skill cooldowns are drastically reduced, and summon gauge generation is accelerated by 200%.

The ultimate objective during Link Time is to trigger a **Primal Burst**. This is achieved when all four party members execute their Skybound Arts (SBA) in sequence while a summon is active on the field.

### Step-by-Step Primal Burst Strategy:
1. **Build the Link Gauge:** Use multi-hit characters like [Beatrix](/en/characters/beatrix-guide/) or Lancelot to quickly build the Link Gauge to 100%.
2. **Trigger Link Time:** Coordinate the final Link Attack. Ensure all players are in position.
3. **Initiate the Summon:** The designated initiator summons Bahamut or Lucilius.
4. **Execute SBA Chain:** Player 1 activates their SBA immediately. This freezes the boss's action frame.
5. **Chain SBAs:** Players 2, 3, and 4 chain their SBAs sequentially (Full Chain).
6. **Primal Burst Trigger:** Upon completion of the Full Chain, the active summon absorbs the elemental energy, executing a Primal Burst that deals massive, non-elemental damage and inflicts a 10-second Slow status on the boss.

## FAQ: Multiplayer Summoning in Endless Ragnarok

### Can two players summon Lucilius at the same time?
No. Under the **Endless Ragnarok one summon at a time co op** rule, only one summon can be active on the field at any given time. If one player has summoned Lucilius, all other players' summon prompts will be locked until Lucilius's duration expires or his ultimate skill is triggered.

### Does the subsequent summon discount apply if the previous summon was interrupted?
Yes. As long as the active summon's duration bar empties or is completed via an ultimate skill, the 5-second Resonance Window will trigger. However, if the summoning player is knocked out before the summon animation completes, the summon is canceled, no discount is applied, and the SAP is refunded to the player.

### Can I summon during a boss's overdrive phase?
Yes, you can summon during overdrive. In fact, summoning Rolan during Beelzebub's or The World's overdrive phases is a highly recommended strategy, as Rolan's defensive barriers can protect the party from devastating arena-wide attacks.

### How do Master Traits affect summon mechanics?
Master Traits (Insight, Essence, and Crux paths unlocked at Level 100) provide passive boosts to your character's stats, but certain nodes also enhance summon performance. For example, the *Insight* path for support characters like Maglielle increases the active duration of summoned beasts by up to 10%, while the *Crux* path for DPS characters like Beatrix increases the damage dealt during a Primal Burst.
