The Conflux stands as the premier solo roguelite endgame loop introduced in the Endless Ragnarok expansion for *Granblue Fantasy: Relink*. Accessible via the glowing dimensional gateways located in either Seedhollow or the Tredame Sanctum, this mode challenges players to descend through 20 depths of randomized combat encounters, trap rooms, and boss fights. To optimize your **Endless Ragnarok Conflux farming** runs, you must understand how to manipulate room choices, stack the correct temporary buffs, and configure speedrun-oriented builds. Players looking to master **Endless Ragnarok Conflux farming** will find that run times can be cut down from 40 minutes to under 15 minutes with the right setups.

To unlock the Conflux, you must first complete the Proud Quest "The Tale of Bahamut's Rage" to unlock the Epilogue. Once unlocked, the Conflux becomes your primary pipeline for securing Weapon Transcendence materials, S-tier Sigils, and the Master Trait points required to max out all 28 characters. This guide outlines the fastest routing strategies, builds, and mechanics to maximize your farming efficiency.

## Gateway Selection and Room Mechanics

Before initiating a Conflux run, you must choose between the Seedhollow Gateway and the Tredame Sanctum Gateway. While both lead to the same pool of 20 depths, they feature distinct baseline modifiers that influence the types of rooms that spawn. 

The Seedhollow Gateway biases generation toward high-density Combat Rooms, making it the preferred choice for players seeking fast mob clears and raw experience points. Conversely, the Tredame Sanctum Gateway biases generation toward Treasure Rooms and Rare Monster Rooms, which is ideal when your primary goal is high-tier Sigil acquisition.

Inside the Conflux, you will encounter three primary room types:
*   **Combat Rooms**: Standard arenas where you must defeat waves of enemies. Clearing these quickly yields basic upgrade materials and minor buffs.
*   **Treasure Rooms**: Non-combat rooms containing chests of varying rarities (Silver, Gold, and Rainbow). These rooms are essential for obtaining Curios and High-Tier Sigils.
*   **Rare Monster Rooms**: Combat rooms featuring unique mini-bosses, such as Arcarum Mimics or elite Heralds of Doom. These rooms offer the highest drop rates for Transcendence materials but carry a high risk of failure.

| Room Type | Primary Objective | Key Drops | Spawn Probability (Seedhollow) | Spawn Probability (Tredame) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Combat Room** | Defeat all enemy waves | Weapon Refines, Fortitude Crystals | 65% | 45% |
| **Treasure Room** | Open chests within a time limit | Curios, A-Tier Sigils, Gold Dalia Badges | 15% | 30% |
| **Rare Monster Room** | Defeat the elite mini-boss | Conflux Cores, S-Tier Sigils, Fatebreaker Keys | 20% | 25% |

Before entering each depth, you are presented with a choice of three random power-ups. These power-ups remain active until you either complete Depth 20 or exit the run. For speedrunning, prioritize offensive multipliers such as Critical Hit Rate, Skill Damage, and Link Temp Gain over defensive options like Defense Up or HP Regen.

## Fastest Run Routes and Timing Benchmarks

Achieving an **Endless Ragnarok Conflux fastest run** requires strict pathing discipline. You should aim to complete a full 20-depth run in under 15 minutes. To do this, you must bypass unnecessary combat encounters and prioritize rooms that can be cleared in under 45 seconds.

During the early depths (1–5), focus entirely on stacking offensive power-ups. Do not waste time in Treasure Rooms early on, as the chests in these depths have lower rarity caps. In the middle depths (6–15), transition your focus to Rare Monster Rooms to secure critical **Endless Ragnarok Conflux materials**. In the final depths (16–20), prioritize Treasure Rooms to maximize your chances of obtaining S-tier Sigils, before preparing for the final boss encounter at Depth 20.

| Depth Range | Primary Target Room | Target Clear Time | Key Milestones & Buff Priorities |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Depths 1–5** | Combat Room | < 30 seconds per room | Stack Attack Power and Critical Hit Rate buffs. |
| **Depths 6–10** | Rare Monster Room | < 60 seconds per room | Secure at least two Conflux Cores; acquire Link Temp buffs. |
| **Depths 11–15** | Combat Room (Horde) | < 45 seconds per room | Avoid single-target boss rooms; focus on AoE clear. |
| **Depths 16–19** | Treasure Room | < 20 seconds per room | Open Rainbow Chests; bypass optional combat where possible. |
| **Depth 20** | Boss Arena (Beelzebub/The World) | < 120 seconds | Trigger Primal Burst immediately during Link Time. |

When executing an **Endless Ragnarok Conflux fastest run**, avoid "Survival" objectives where you must wait out a timer. If a Survival objective is unavoidable, use characters with high pull-in capabilities, such as Cagliostro or the DLC character Fraux, to group and destroy enemies instantly to force faster wave spawns.

Beatrix utilizes her Delta Clock mechanic to slow down local enemy actions while ramping up her own physical DPS. ## Material Yields and Weapon Transcendence Integration

The primary incentive for engaging in **Endless Ragnarok Conflux endgame farming** is to gather the materials required for Weapon Transcendence. This system allows you to push fully upgraded weapons (Defender, Ascension, Executioner, Stinger, Stunner, and Terminus) past their initial limits, unlocking swappable traits (Slot II up to level 25, Slot IV up to level 10) and Slot EX rewards.

While Fatebreaker quests provide the base materials for Transcendence, the specialized catalysts required to fuse these upgrades only drop from the deeper levels of the Conflux.

| Material Name | Primary Source | Drop Rate (Depths 15–20) | Transcendence Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Conflux Core** | Rare Monster Rooms | ~35% per run | Level 110–120 Transcendence Cap Unlock |
| **Astral Shard** | Rainbow Chests (Depths 16+) | ~15% per chest | Unlocking Slot II Swappable Traits |
| **Fatebreaker Key** | Depth 20 Boss (Chaos Difficulty) | 100% (Guaranteed) | Unlocking Slot IV Swappable Traits |
| **Ragnarok Decal** | Beelzebub (Rare Spawn) | ~5% per encounter | Aesthetic Weapon Glow Effects |

To optimize your material loop, run the Tredame Sanctum Gateway on Chaos difficulty. Ensure you have unlocked your character's character-specific Sigil+ from the Knickknack Shack before attempting these depths, as the damage cap increase is mandatory for clearing the high-health pools of Depth 20 bosses. For more details on the expansion's release features and character scaling, you can consult the official [Cygames Granblue Fantasy: Relink Product Page](https://relink.granbluefantasy.jp/en/).

## Advanced Strategies for Chaos Difficulty Rooms

Chaos difficulty in the Conflux introduces one-hit kill mechanics from standard mobs. To survive and maintain your clear speed, you must utilize the Summon System effectively. 

Lyria allows the party to equip up to four primal beast summons, though only one may be active on the field at a time. In the solo Conflux mode, you can summon Lucilius or Rolan to clear the arena. Summoning Lucilius provides a temporary field of invincibility while dealing massive Dark damage to all active targets. Rolan, on the other hand, provides a party-wide attack speed buff and executes a coordinated slash that breaks enemy shields instantly.

To maximize your summon efficiency:
1.  **Save Summons for Depth 15 and 20**: Do not waste your summon charge on early depths. Save them for the elite encounters where boss shield phases can stall your run.
2.  **Trigger Primal Burst**: When your SBA gauge is full, initiate your Skybound Art during Link Time. This triggers a Primal Burst, dealing millions of neutral element damage to the boss and skipping phase transitions.
3.  **Utilize Dodge Cancel Mechanics**: Characters like Lancelot and Sandalphon can cancel their recovery animations with a dodge without resetting their combo strings. Use this to maintain your damage output while avoiding the one-hit kill attacks common in Chaos difficulty.

For a complete breakdown of high-difficulty boss attack patterns, see the [Fatebreaker Quests Guide](/en/endgame/fatebreaker-quests-guide/) to prepare for the mechanics you will encounter at Depth 20.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### What is the absolute fastest character build for clearing the Conflux?
Sandalphon utilizing a specialized "SBA Loop" build is currently the fastest setup. By equipping Alpha V+ and Gamma V+ Sigils alongside his character-specific Warpath+ Sigil, Sandalphon can maintain Supreme Primarch Mode indefinitely while generating enough SBA gauge to execute a Skybound Art every 45 seconds, skipping entire boss phases.

### Can I farm War Elemental+ Sigils in the Conflux?
Yes. War Elemental+ Sigils drop exclusively from Rainbow Chests in Treasure Rooms at Depth 18 or higher, and as a rare drop from the Depth 20 boss on Chaos difficulty. To target these, run the Tredame Sanctum Gateway to increase the spawn rate of late-stage Treasure Rooms. For more information on Sigil drop pools, check the [Weapon Transcendence Guide](/en/weapon-transcendence/weapon-transcendence-guide/).

### Does crossplay affect Conflux farming runs?
No. The Conflux is a solo-only roguelite mode, meaning you cannot invite other players to your run. However, the game features full cross-platform play across PS5, PS4, Switch 2, and PC (Steam) for the Fatebreaker co-op quests, which you will need to run to acquire the base materials that complement your Conflux drops.

### How do the new DLC characters perform in Conflux speedruns?
Beatrix and Eustace perform exceptionally well. Beatrix's Delta Clock mechanic is highly effective at neutralizing fast-moving bosses, while Eustace's ranged AoE attacks allow him to clear horde rooms without having to chase down scattered enemies. Maglielle is also highly viable due to her automated flying weapons, which allow you to focus entirely on dodging hazards in Chaos difficulty.
