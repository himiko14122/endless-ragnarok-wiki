## Unlocking Weapon Transcendence: Prerequisites and Quests

Before you can begin upgrading your arsenal, you must meet several progression milestones. The system is locked behind the expansion's endgame campaign and high-difficulty quest clears. You cannot access Transcendence menu options at the Blacksmith until these steps are completed.

First, you must complete the Proud-difficulty quest "The Tale of Bahamut's Rage" to unlock the Endless Ragnarok Epilogue. Once the Epilogue is complete, you will gain access to Chaos-difficulty quests at the Quest Counter in Seedhollow or Folca. 

To officially unlock the Transcendence system, you must complete your first Fatebreaker quest. Fatebreaker quests are specialized, high-difficulty boss fights featuring enemies like Ragnalia (Heralds of Doom) and the duo boss fight against Seofon & Tweyen. Clearing these quests rewards you with Fatebreaker Sigils and unlocks the "Transcend Weapon" option at the Blacksmith.

## Weapon Types and Transcendence Level Caps

The **Endless Ragnarok Weapon Transcendence explained** loop applies to all six weapon categories in the game. Unlike the base game where Terminus weapons were the undisputed best-in-slot option for every character, Transcendence balances the playing field. By upgrading non-Terminus weapons, players can unlock unique stat distributions and utility traits that rival or exceed base Terminus performance.

The six weapon types eligible for Transcendence are:
*   **Defender:** Focused on massive HP pools and defensive utility.
*   **Ascension:** Balanced stats with healing and attack multipliers.
*   **Executioner:** High weak-point damage and break capabilities.
*   **Stinger:** Critical hit rate and critical damage amplification.
*   **Stunner:** High stun power to trigger Link Attacks faster.
*   **Terminus:** Ultimate offensive weapons with specialized damage cap increases.

Transcendence is divided into five distinct stages, with each stage raising the weapon's level cap by 10 levels. Reaching Level 200 unlocks the weapon's ultimate aesthetic effect (a unique elemental glow) and maximum trait slots.

## Swappable Traits and Slot EX Mechanics

One of the most powerful aspects of this system is the introduction of **Endless Ragnarok swappable traits**. When you transcend a weapon to Stage 1, you unlock Slot II. Reaching Stage 4 unlocks Slot IV. These slots allow you to customize your weapon with passive traits that can be swapped out at the Blacksmith using refinement materials.

Slot II and Slot IV have different maximum level caps for their equipped traits:
*   **Slot II:** Can hold traits up to a maximum of Level 25.
*   **Slot IV:** Can hold traits up to a maximum of Level 10.

These traits function independently of your equipped Sigils, allowing you to easily reach the game's hard stat caps without sacrificing valuable Sigil slots. For example, placing a Level 25 Critical Hit Rate trait in Slot II frees up an S-tier Sigil slot on characters like [Sandalphon](/en/characters/sandalphon-guide/) or the DLC character [Beatrix](/en/characters/beatrix-guide/).

### Table 3: Popular Swappable Traits and Level Caps

| Trait Name | Slot Compatibility | Max Level | Effect at Max Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Critical Hit Rate | Slot II | Level 25 | +25% Critical Hit Rate |
| Damage Cap | Slot II | Level 25 | +25% Damage Cap |
| Stun Power | Slot II | Level 25 | +50 Stun Power |
| War Elemental (Mini) | Slot IV | Level 10 | Deals 10% extra damage as superior element |
| Cascade | Slot IV | Level 10 | Reduces skill cooldowns by up to 1.5% on hit |
| Quick Cooldown | Slot IV | Level 10 | Reduces skill cooldowns by 10% |

In addition to swappable traits, players unlock **Slot EX Rewards** at Stage 3 and Stage 5. These are permanent, passive bonuses tied to the weapon itself. Unlike swappable traits, Slot EX rewards cannot be changed, but they provide universal buffs such as Skybound Art (SBA) Damage Cap Up (+10%) and Link Time Attack Up (+15%).

## Transcendence Materials and Farming Routes

Upgrading your weapons requires a mix of materials from endgame activities. To meet the steep **Endless Ragnarok Weapon Transcendence requirements**, you must divide your time between high-difficulty Fatebreaker quests and [The Conflux](/en/conflux/the-conflux-complete-guide/).

[The Conflux](/en/conflux/the-conflux-complete-guide/) is a solo roguelite mode accessible via gateways in Seedhollow or the Tredame Sanctum. Runs take approximately 20 minutes. Deeper depths within The Conflux yield Ragnarok Pebbles and Conflux Orbs, which are mandatory for Stage 3 through Stage 5 upgrades.

Fatebreaker quests, on the other hand, drop boss-specific materials. For instance, upgrading a Terminus weapon requires Beelzebub's Dark Matter, while upgrading a Defender weapon requires Arcarum Cores dropped by The World.

If you want to know **Endless Ragnarok how to transcend weapons** efficiently, follow this farming rotation:
1.  Run [The Conflux](/en/conflux/the-conflux-complete-guide/) to farm Ragnarok Pebbles and Refinement Sand.
2.  Clear the weekly Fatebreaker quest rotation for specialized boss drops.
3.  Trade excess materials at Sierokart’s Knickknack Shack for Weapon Transcendence Catalysts.

*   **Fraux:** As a melee pactbearer to The Devil, Fraux benefits from transcending her Executioner weapon to maximize break damage during Link Time.

When optimizing your build, ensure your **Endless Ragnarok swappable traits** complement your S-tier Sigils. If you are running War Elemental+ and Warpath+ Sigils, use your weapon's Slot II for Critical Hit Rate V+ and Slot IV for Quick Cooldown to maximize skill uptime.

## FAQ: Weapon Transcendence Questions Answered

### What is the fastest way to get Ragnarok Pebbles for Weapon Transcendence?
The fastest source of Ragnarok Pebbles is [The Conflux](/en/conflux/the-conflux-complete-guide/) roguelite mode. Focus on clearing depths 15 through 30, as these areas have a higher drop rate for rare enhancement materials. Using power-ups that increase drop rates before entering combat rooms will also speed up your farm.

### Can you roll character-specific Sigil traits in swappable slots?
No, character-specific traits (such as Sandalphon's Supreme Primarch utility or Beatrix's Delta Clock extensions) cannot be rolled as **Endless Ragnarok swappable traits** in Slot II or Slot IV. These slots are reserved for general traits like Critical Hit Rate, Damage Cap, Quick Cooldown, and Stun Power. Character-specific traits must still be equipped via S-tier Sigils.

### Does Weapon Transcendence carry over if I switch weapons?
No. Weapon Transcendence is tied to the individual weapon, not the character. If you transcend Sandalphon's Ascension weapon to level 200, his Terminus weapon will remain at its current level until you separately invest the required materials to transcend it. Because of this, it is highly recommended to plan your build and commit to one weapon per character first.

### What are the minimum requirements to start transcending weapons?
The minimum **Endless Ragnarok Weapon Transcendence requirements** are completing the Proud-difficulty quest "The Tale of Bahamut's Rage", unlocking Chaos difficulty, and clearing at least one Fatebreaker quest to obtain Fatebreaker Sigils. Your target weapon must also be fully enhanced to level 150 before the Transcendence option becomes available at the Blacksmith.
