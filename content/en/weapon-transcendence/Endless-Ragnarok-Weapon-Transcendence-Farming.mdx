Unlocking the ultimate potential of your arsenal in the Endless Ragnarok expansion requires navigating the Weapon Transcendence system. This end-game progression mechanic allows players to upgrade fully enhanced weapons—including Defender, Ascension, Executioner, Stinger, Stunner, and Terminus types—well beyond their initial limits. To achieve this, you must gather rare materials scattered across high-difficulty game modes. This guide details the most efficient strategies for **Weapon Transcendence Farming — How to Get Materials Fast**, comparing solo roguelite runs to team-based boss encounters.

Before you can begin farming **Endless Ragnarok Weapon Transcendence materials**, you must complete the Proud-difficulty quest "The Tale of Bahamut's Rage" to unlock the Epilogue. Completing this unlocks Chaos-difficulty quests and opens the gateways to both the Fatebreaker quests and The Conflux. Maximizing your farming efficiency requires a clear understanding of where specific materials drop and how to optimize your character builds to clear these challenges in record time.

## Weapon Transcendence Materials and Sources

Weapon Transcendence introduces several new materials required to break the level caps of your weapons, unlock swappable traits (Slot II up to level 25, Slot IV up to level 10), and claim Slot EX rewards. These materials are split between two primary activities: The Conflux and Fatebreaker quests. 

Knowing exactly which material is required for each upgrade step prevents wasted time in inefficient queues. The primary materials include Ragnarok Spheres, Fatebreaker Shards, Conflux Essences, and Astral Tears.

| Material Name | Rarity | Primary Source | Used For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Ragnarok Sphere** | Legendary | Fatebreaker Quests (Chaos Difficulty) | Level 105–120 Transcendence Cap Breaks |
| **Conflux Essence** | Epic | The Conflux (Depths 15+) | Slot II Trait Swapping & Level Upgrades |
| **Fatebreaker Shard** | Epic | Fatebreaker Quests (All Difficulties) | Slot IV Trait Swapping & Level Upgrades |
| **Astral Tear** | Legendary | Beelzebub / The World Boss Drops | Slot EX Reward Unlocks |
| **Chaos Grimoire** | Rare | Chaos Difficulty Quests (General Drop) | Base Transcendence Leveling |

Each weapon type requires a specific combination of these materials. For instance, Terminus weapons require a higher ratio of Ragnarok Spheres and Astral Tears compared to Defender or Ascension weapons, making targeted boss farming essential.

## Conflux Runs vs. Fatebreaker Quests: Efficiency Analysis

To determine how to get materials fast, players must choose between the solo-only roguelite mode, [The Conflux](/en/conflux/the-conflux-complete-guide/), and the multiplayer-compatible Fatebreaker quests. Both offer distinct advantages depending on your gear level, character selection, and whether you are playing solo or in a coordinated group.

### The Conflux: Solo Roguelite Farming

The Conflux is accessed via gateways in Seedhollow or the Tredame Sanctum. Runs last approximately 20 minutes and consist of randomly generated combat, treasure, and rare monster rooms. You select power-ups before entering each area, which remain active until you exit or fail the run. 

*   **Pros:** Fastest source of **Endless Ragnarok Conflux materials for weapons** (Conflux Essences); highly consistent solo farming; no matchmaking wait times.
*   **Cons:** Cannot be played in co-op; requires high individual skill; rewards scale down if you fail before Depth 20.

For players focusing on solo play, characters like [Sandalphon](/en/characters/sandalphon-guide/) excel in this mode due to his Supreme Primarch Mode and lightstep sword wave spam, which easily clears crowded mob rooms.

### Fatebreaker Quests: High-Difficulty Boss Raids

Fatebreaker quests are targeted boss fights against major antagonists such as Beelzebub, The World, Ragnalia, and the duo boss fight featuring Seofon & Tweyen. These quests are the exclusive source of **Endless Ragnarok Fatebreaker quests materials**, such as Fatebreaker Shards and Ragnarok Spheres.

*   **Pros:** Supports full cross-platform play (PS5, PS4, Switch 2, PC); highly predictable drop tables; quick clears with a coordinated team.
*   **Cons:** High entry barrier; Chaos difficulty bosses can easily one-hit undergeared characters; public matchmaking can be inconsistent.

| Metric | The Conflux (Depth 15-25) | Fatebreaker Quests (Chaos) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Average Run Time** | 18–22 minutes | 4–7 minutes (Co-op) |
| **Primary Material Yield** | 8-12 Conflux Essences | 2-4 Fatebreaker Shards, 1-2 Ragnarok Spheres |
| **Difficulty Level** | Scaling (Moderate to High) | Static (Very High / Chaos) |
| **Best Used For** | Slot II Trait Upgrades & EX Materials | Cap Breaking & Slot IV Trait Upgrades |

## Step-by-Step Fatebreaker Quest Farming Routes

To optimize your **Endless Ragnarok weapon farming** route, you should target specific Fatebreaker quests depending on which boss mechanics your team can bypass the fastest. The three most efficient boss fights for farming Transcendence materials are Beelzebub, The World, and Ragnalia.

### Route 1: The Beelzebub Speedrun (Ragnarok Spheres & Astral Tears)

Beelzebub is the fastest boss to farm if your party utilizes high-DPS characters capable of skipping his phase transitions. 

1.  **Party Setup:** Bring at least one support character like Maglielle to provide shields and flying weapon distraction. The remaining slots should feature high-damage dealers like Beatrix (utilizing her Delta Clock mechanic for rapid burst) or Vaseraga.
2.  **Phase 1 (100% - 70% HP):** Focus on building the Link Gauge. Avoid using Skybound Arts (SBA) individually; save them for Link Time.
3.  **Phase 2 (Chaos Legion at 70%):** When Beelzebub casts Chaos Legion, immediately deploy a defensive summon like Rolan to mitigate the arena-wide shockwaves. 
4.  **Phase 3 (Link Time Burst):** Trigger Link Time immediately after Chaos Legion ends. Chain all four SBAs to activate a Primal Burst. This should deplete the remaining 70% of his health pool, bypassing his final phase entirely.

### Route 2: The World Loop (Fatebreaker Shards)

The World is an Arcarum creation that features predictable, slow-moving AoE attacks, making it the safest boss to farm for Fatebreaker Shards.

1.  **Party Setup:** Utilize Eustace for ranged paralysis and Fediel to place Dark fields that protect the party from status ailments.
2.  **Strategy:** Keep Eustace at a distance to constantly apply stun value. When The World attempts to cast its screen-wide laser grid, use Eustace’s paralyzing shots to interrupt the channel.
3.  **Reward Optimization:** Break the boss's head and core parts before defeating it to secure bonus drop slots for Fatebreaker Shards.

| **Beelzebub** | *Chaos Descent* | Ragnarok Sphere | 35% (Double on MVP) |

## Loadout Optimization for Speed Clears

To achieve fast clear times in Chaos-difficulty quests, your party's loadouts must be optimized with specific Sigils and Summons. Survival is just as important as damage, as a single down can ruin your chances of achieving an S++ rank, which increases material drop rates.

### Recommended Sigil Configuration

Prioritize S-tier and A-tier Sigils that boost your damage cap and provide utility. 

*   **Warpath+ & War Elemental+:** These S-tier Sigils are mandatory for bypassing standard damage caps and ensuring your attacks always hit the enemy's elemental weakness.
*   **Alpha, Beta, Gamma Sigils:** Equipping these A-tier Sigils enhances your SBA damage and generates shield barriers upon SBA execution, keeping your team alive during boss phase transitions.
*   **Character-Specific Sigils:** Ensure your active character has their unique double-trait Sigil equipped to maximize their core mechanic (e.g., Beatrix's Delta Clock extension).

### Summon System Integration

In Chaos difficulty, Lyria can summon powerful primal beasts. Each player can bring up to four summons, but only one can be active on the field at a time.

*   **Lucilius Summon:** Provides massive passive attack boosts and can be controlled directly to deal heavy light elemental damage, which is highly effective against Beelzebub.
*   **Rolan Summon:** Deploys defensive barriers and healing zones, allowing your DPS characters to ignore minor boss attacks and maintain their damage rotations.
*   **Primal Burst:** Coordinate with your team to trigger your SBAs during Link Time. Having all four party members execute their SBAs simultaneously triggers a Primal Burst, dealing millions of points of elemental damage and stalling the boss's action bar.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I farm Weapon Transcendence materials in multiplayer?
Yes. Fatebreaker quests support full crossplay across PS5, PS4, Switch 2, and PC. You can queue with friends or random players to clear these high-difficulty boss fights. However, The Conflux is a solo-only roguelite mode, meaning all Conflux Essences must be farmed individually.

### Which weapon type should I transcend first?
For almost all characters, the **Terminus Weapon** should be your first priority for Transcendence. Terminus weapons receive the largest stat scaling from Transcendence levels and unlock the most powerful Slot EX traits. Defender weapons can be transcended if you are struggling with survivability in Chaos difficulty.

### How do I unlock the Fatebreaker quests?
You must first complete the base game's main story and the Proud-difficulty quest "The Tale of Bahamut's Rage." Once completed, the Epilogue of the Endless Ragnarok DLC will unlock. Progressing through the Epilogue quests will grant you access to Chaos difficulty and the Fatebreaker quest nodes at the Quest Counter.

### What is the fastest way to get Conflux Essences?
The fastest method to obtain Conflux Essences is to run The Conflux solo, targeting Depth 15 to 25. Focus on selecting combat rooms that feature "Rare Monsters," as they drop guaranteed Essences upon defeat. Avoid treasure rooms if your sole goal is material farming, as they have lower essence drop rates.
